Transferring a domain name in use with iCloud custom domain

4 September 2024

Years ago I purchased a domain name so I could have a customised e-mail address through Microsoft 365 Personal. The registrar was... Godaddy. YES, I know: in my defence, it was Microsoft's default registrar for personal domain names, so I just went with it. Last year though, out of the bluest blue and for absolutely incomprehensible reasons, Microsoft decided to deprecate and retire the customised domain name functionality from 365 Personal. As I pay for an Apple One Premium plan, I decided to make use of the included Custom E-mail Domain feature and re-created the same e-mail address through iCloud. The onboarding process was buttery-smooth: I entered my domain name, Apple realised it was registered and managed through Godaddy, and asked me permission to edit the DNS records so they could reflect the proper values in order for the feature to work; I granted it, things happened behind the scenes and before I knew it, the procedure was over and I was left with the same e-mail address I had previously, only through iCloud.

You'll probably guess where I'm going: about a week ago I realised that renewal season was approaching. Also, I realised that Godaddy is expensive, compared to other, more nimble yet apparently reliable registrars. That's why I decided to transfer my domain to Porkbun. Websites and domain names aren't my bread-and-butter though, so I obviously asked myself what next: was I supposed to deactivate the Custom E-mail Domain feature, transfer the domain, do whatever I had to do with the DNS records, then set up the feature again? But what if by whatever Apple quirk I couldn't recreate or I lost access to the address? Maybe copying the DNS records during the transfer would have been enough?

Apparently, iCloud Mail with custom domain names uses five DNS records: two MX, one CNAME (for DKIM) and two TXT. Their values are the same for all iCloud Mail accounts, except for one of those TXT records, which contains a personal value that's unique for each custom domain. I obviously knew my personal value, since it was right there in Godaddy's DNS dashboard. I looked around Reddit and personal blogs to see if other people had anything to say about similar experiences: in one Reddit thread, a user reported that none of the values were registrar-dependent, so in theory one could just copy them over to the new registrar during the transfer procedure while trying to have as little downtime as possible. Separately, I posted on a Google user group (they still exist!!) asking for advice, but either I didn't explain myself clearly or I think they had no idea how the iCloud feature worked, since they argued that I would've had to set up everything again; maybe they thought that Apple directly manages those domain names? Who knows.

Long story short: I purchased the transfer on a Saturday, copied the required DNS records and values from Godaddy to Porkbun (including the personal TXT value; the KB guide is quite clear in saying what goes where, though it doesn't mention this specific aspect), followed their suggestions so I could minimise the DNS downtime, and manually released the outgoing transfer from Godaddy. After waiting about 24 hours and sending a few test e-mails to mail-tester.com (if you use a custom domain for e-mail: do it!), by Sunday evening everything worked flawlessly without disabling and setting up anything on Apple's side.

So, in case you use iCloud Mail with a custom domain and you want to transfer the domain name to a new registrar: just copy and paste the DNS records. You'll be fine, I guess. At least, it went fine for me, so... 🤷
