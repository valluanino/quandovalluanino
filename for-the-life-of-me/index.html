For the life of me...

7 July 2021

... I don't know how people who live an exaggerated amount of time zones away (like, think UTC-8 compared to UTC+2, basically CEST) can keep up with stuff that happens on the other side of the world and seem so comfortable doing that to boot. This comes from a guy who finds Skype calls almost unbearably stressful (especially if only audio is involved) and has ignored Skype calls in the past because the only thing he could think while receiving the call was “Oh god...”, so take my rant with a grain of salt.

Like, there's this guy I know who, guess what, lives somewhere like UTC-8 or -9 (I don't feel like checking, and I also don't want to be too specific). Once in a while, wow, he starts posting stuff and getting genuinely interested about life at his old place, which coincidentally is my everyday place, like he still lives there. What gives? And most of all: how can you maintain relationships so tightly and so comfortably that you suddenly feel like you should start shilling for local political candidates? Like, you haven't lived here for ages, and every time you come you behave like a tourist, this is clearly not entirely your place anymore, yet you don't feel... weird suggesting people how to vote in -again, sorry for repeating, a very local election? And it's just because you know the guy?

So, you get to tell people who they should vote for major of a town you live, like, 8 time zones away from because you skype the guy, and I can't even get myself to ask “How have you been doing?” to a guy in my neighbouring country 'cause I feel like I'm invading and I get anxious?... holy shit!

Yeah, I know it's just me and not him, but it's been bothering me tonight. That's why I'm rambling.
