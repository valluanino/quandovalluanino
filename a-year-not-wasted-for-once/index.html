A year not wasted, for once

4 December 2018

(this was originally posted elsewhere on October 1st, 2018)

Up until four years ago, I was totally sedentary. This was also due to an old ankle injury (we’re talking about 2007-ish) that made me feel less confident in doing any kind of physical activity. And by the way it still hurts sometimes, and I still can’t bend the right ankle past a certain degree – but still.

Four years ago was when I was introduced to the idea of going to a gym. Mind you: I’m not a gym buff, quite the opposite in fact. It wasn’t something I did with passion: it was just a new routine, something that helped me unwind. Time passed, and I realised that something was missing. When the benefits grinded to a halt and started actually reverting, I knew I had to change something. I asked myself what was it that I was doing wrong, and I think (post-hoc) I figured it out.

As I said, I’m not a gym buff. In fact, I tend not to be social at all. I’m not an extrovert and I’d rather not take initiatives. Still, in all my fondest memories I’m never alone. I may not be the protagonist in most of them, I may be in the background more often than not, but I’m still there- with people. There’s no point in breaking the lonely routine being lonely somewhere else (in my case, the gym). Being comfortable with myself and doing it for my own sake wasn’t cutting it anymore. After many, many years, I felt, for once, the need to reach out, integrating my own, personal motivation with those of other people. I felt I needed to be at peace with others in order to be at peace with myself.

It’s been a year since then. Last October I signed up for a functional training course. A year later, I can say it was there that I realised I was correct in my analysis. This newfound spark brought better daily habits and both psychological and physical benefits: I go out much more often, even for a simple walk; I’m less moody and more tranquil; I managed to lose 10 kg in 5 months just by eating less- no super-extra-fancy special weight-loss diet needed (and trust me, a year ago losing 10 kg would have been one of my wildest dreams).

What I learned is that finding the right motivation is fundamental. Tech gizmos, like heart-rate monitors, smart wearables and calorie-counting apps, do help when trying to reach a certain goal, but without the right mindset you risk not even seeing it from afar. And if you lose your motivation, it’s probably because you’re changing. The drive you had when you started got replaced with a new one: failing to take notice can be easy and it always brings disorientation and, eventually, dispassion. I managed to noticed this change in time and tried to foster it, letting it suggest me what to do next.

And hey, it worked. A year later I can say, for once and after many disappointments, that this year wasn’t wasted.
